About UMIC

Unmesh Mashruwala Innovation Cell, also widely known as UMIC, is a tech team formed in the late 90s to introduce the field of robotics to students. The team consists of 50+ diligent and dedicated students from various fields of study from IIT Bombay working towards embracing and developing the idea of an autonomous future. UMIC works on the principle of co-existence of Machines and Humans developing automated prototypes to make the world a safer and more sustainable place. Under the sheds of UMIC, the primary area of focus is developing autonomous vehicles.

AeRoVe

On a never-ending pursuit of developing an ultimate system of autonomous multirotor and fixed-wing aircraft.


At Team AeRoVe, we always look for problems that challenge, excite, and motivate us to grow intellectually. Our purpose is to further the boundaries of autonomous aerial technology and realize the true potential of this field. We aim to contribute and start the culture of creating cutting edge technology through indigenous innovation.

Incorporating different subsystems namely ‘Mechatronics’, ‘Avionics','Controls’, ‘Machine Learning’, ‘Localisation’, ‘Path Planning’, and ‘Localisation’, the team emphatically covers every aspect to forge a de rigueur system of autonomous aerial vehicles. We have been working on the development of a multirotor and a fixed-wing aircraft, both of which are capable of autonomous flight.

SeDriCa

On a mission to achieve Level 5 autonomy, capable of autonomous driving under Indian road conditions


Autonomous cars have always been a dream, and the continuous advancements in technology are bringing us a step closer to this dream every day. We, at SeDriCa, have been living with this dream in our minds for long and have been working tirelessly towards achieving it.

Our team is in an endless pursuit of innovation in autonomy and incorporates a wide array of cutting-edge technologies, right from detection models and mapping techniques to complex planning and control algorithms. Apart from that, we also use best-in-class sensors such as LiDARs, cameras, and IMU to gather data from the vehicle's surroundings to build India’s first self-driving car.
